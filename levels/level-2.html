KIIP Level 2 — Grammar & Vocabulary

A self-study reference for 한국어와 한국문화 초급 2 (18-unit edition) · Korea Immigration & Integration Program

This reference follows the KIIP Level 2 textbook (한국어와 한국문화 초급 2) — the high-beginner stage of the Social Integration Program. It is organized into the book's 18 units. Each unit introduces its everyday topic, the two grammar patterns taught in it (with form, meaning, and example sentences), and the matching vocabulary. A grammar index closes the book. This builds directly on the particles and basic endings learned in Level 1.

How to read each entry

V
Verb (동사) — e.g. 가다, 먹다
A
Adjective / descriptive verb (형용사) — e.g. 크다, 예쁘다
N
Noun (명사) — e.g. 사람, 학교
-(으)
Use -으 after a consonant-final stem; drop it after a vowel-final stem
아 / 어
Choose 아 after ㅏ/ㅗ stem vowels, otherwise 어 (하다 → 해)

UNIT 1고향 소개Introducing Your Hometown

N + (이)라고 하다 — "to be called / named"

Form consonant-final N + 이라고 하다 · vowel-final N + 라고 하다

Used to state a name or what something is called, including when introducing yourself.

제 고향은 부산이라고 해요.

My hometown is called Busan.

저는 흐엉이라고 해요.

I'm called Huong.

A + (으)ㄴ + N — "adjective describing a noun"

Form consonant adj-stem + 은 · vowel adj-stem + ㄴ, before a noun

Turns an adjective into a modifier placed before a noun ("a beautiful place").

경치가 아름다운 곳이에요.

It's a place with beautiful scenery.

조용한 동네에 살아요.

I live in a quiet neighborhood.

UNIT 2집안일Doing Housework

V + (으)ㄹ게요 — "I'll (do) — offer / intention"

Form consonant stem + 을게요 · vowel stem + ㄹ게요 (1st person only)

Expresses the speaker's will or a promise/offer, responding to the situation or listener.

쓰레기는 제가 버릴게요.

I'll take out the trash.

제가 설거지를 할게요.

I'll do the dishes.

V + (으)ㄴ 다음에 — "after doing"

Form consonant stem + 은 다음에 · vowel stem + ㄴ 다음에 (= 후에)

Says that the second action happens after the first is finished.

청소한 다음에 쉴 거예요.

I'll rest after cleaning.

밥을 먹은 다음에 약을 드세요.

Take the medicine after eating.

UNIT 3옷, 신발Trying On Clothes

N + (이)나 — "or"

Form consonant-final N + 이나 · vowel-final N + 나

Joins two nouns as alternatives ("A or B"). (After verbs, use -거나.)

치마나 바지를 입어요.

I wear a skirt or pants.

커피나 차를 마셔요.

I drink coffee or tea.

V + 아 / 어 보다 — "try doing"

Form ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아 보다 · other stems + 어 보다

Means to try / attempt an action. In the past (아/어 봤다) it means "have done before".

이거 한번 입어 보세요.

Try wearing this.

한국 음식을 먹어 봤어요.

I've tried Korean food.

UNIT 4전화Making a Phone Call

V + (으)ㄹ 수 있다 / 없다 — "can / cannot"

Form consonant stem + 을 수 있다/없다 · vowel stem + ㄹ 수 있다/없다

Expresses ability or possibility (있다) and inability or impossibility (없다).

지금 통화할 수 있어요?

Can you talk now?

한국 노래를 부를 수 있어요.

I can sing Korean songs.

반말 — "informal (casual) speech"

Form drop 요 from the 아/어요 form (먹어요 → 먹어 · 가요 → 가)

Casual speech used between close friends and to those younger. Use polite speech with strangers and elders.

내일 같이 갈래?

Want to go together tomorrow?

밥 먹었어?

Did you eat?

UNIT 5약, 증상Medicine & Illness

A/V + (으)면 — "if / when"

Form consonant stem + 으면 · vowel stem + 면

Sets up a condition — "if / when X (happens), then Y".

많이 아프면 이 약을 드세요.

If it hurts a lot, take this medicine.

시간이 있으면 같이 가요.

If you have time, let's go together.

V + 아 / 어서 — "because / and then"

Form ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아서 · other stems + 어서 · 하다 → 해서

Links a reason to its result, or two closely-connected actions in sequence. No tense on the first clause.

감기에 걸려서 병원에 갔어요.

I went to the hospital because I caught a cold.

친구를 만나서 같이 밥을 먹었어요.

I met my friend and we ate together.

UNIT 6기분, 감정Feelings & Emotions

A/V + 겠 — "will / I bet (intention, conjecture)"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 겠 + ending (-겠어요, -겠습니다)

Shows the speaker's intention/will, or a guess about a situation.

내일부터 열심히 하겠어요.

I'll work hard from tomorrow.

시험이 어렵겠어요.

The exam looks like it'll be hard.

A/V + (으)ㄹ 때 — "when"

Form consonant stem + 을 때 · vowel stem + ㄹ 때 (past: -았/었을 때)

Marks the time or occasion when something happens.

맛있는 음식을 먹을 때 행복해요.

I'm happy when I eat delicious food.

어렸을 때 시골에서 살았어요.

When I was young, I lived in the countryside.

UNIT 7초대와 방문Invitations & Visiting

V + (으)ㄹ래요? — "would you like to…?"

Form consonant stem + 을래요? · vowel stem + ㄹ래요?

Casually asks the listener's intention or makes a friendly suggestion; as a statement, expresses one's own wish.

같이 저녁 먹을래요?

Would you like to have dinner together?

저는 집에서 쉴래요.

I think I'll rest at home.

A/V + (으)니까 — "because / since"

Form consonant stem + 으니까 · vowel stem + 니까

Gives a reason — and unlike -아/어서, it CAN be followed by a command or suggestion.

집들이니까 휴지를 가져갈게요.

Since it's a housewarming, I'll bring tissues.

비가 오니까 우산을 가져가세요.

It's raining, so take an umbrella.

UNIT 8한국어 수업 신청Signing Up for Class

V + 기 전에 — "before doing"

Form Verb stem + 기 전에 · Noun + 전에

Says the main action happens before the stated action or time.

수업을 듣기 전에 신청해요.

I register before taking the class.

자기 전에 이를 닦아요.

I brush my teeth before sleeping.

V + 기로 하다 — "decide / agree to do"

Form Verb stem + 기로 하다 (past 기로 했다 = decided)

Expresses a decision or an agreement to do something.

9월부터 한국어 수업을 듣기로 했어요.

I decided to take Korean classes from September.

친구하고 주말에 만나기로 했어요.

I arranged to meet my friend on the weekend.

UNIT 9맛, 식당Favorite Restaurants

A/V + (으)ㄹ 것 같다 — "seems / probably"

Form 을/ㄹ 것 같다 (guess about future) · 는/(으)ㄴ 것 같다 (present/past)

Expresses a guess or impression — "it seems / looks like / probably".

이 식당은 맛있을 것 같아요.

This restaurant seems like it'll be tasty.

밖에 비가 오는 것 같아요.

It seems to be raining outside.

V + 는 + N — "the N that … (present relative clause)"

Form Verb stem + 는 + Noun

Lets a verb clause describe a following noun, in present tense ("a restaurant I go to").

제가 자주 가는 식당이에요.

It's a restaurant I often go to.

한국어를 배우는 사람이 많아요.

There are many people who learn Korean.

UNIT 10길 안내Giving Directions

A/V + 는데 — "background / and / but"

Form Verb + 는데 · Adjective + -(으)ㄴ데 · N + 인데

Provides background or context before the main clause, or a soft contrast.

시청 옆에 있는데 가까워요.

It's next to City Hall, and it's close.

이 옷이 예쁜데 좀 비싸요.

These clothes are pretty, but a bit expensive.

A/V + 기 때문에 — "because of"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 기 때문에 · Noun + 때문에

States a clear reason or cause. Cannot be followed by a command or suggestion.

길이 막히기 때문에 지하철을 타요.

Because of the traffic, I take the subway.

비 때문에 행사가 취소됐어요.

The event was canceled because of the rain.

UNIT 11명절Celebrating Holidays

A + 게 — "adverb form (in a … way)"

Form Adjective stem + 게 (turns an adjective into an adverb)

Makes an adjective describe how an action is done (맛있다 → 맛있게 "deliciously").

음식을 맛있게 먹었어요.

I ate the food with relish (deliciously).

방을 깨끗하게 청소했어요.

I cleaned the room thoroughly.

V + (으)면서 — "while doing (at the same time)"

Form consonant stem + 으면서 · vowel stem + 면서 (same subject)

Two actions by the same person happening at the same time.

보름달을 보면서 소원을 빌어요.

I make a wish while looking at the full moon.

음악을 들으면서 공부해요.

I study while listening to music.

UNIT 12실수Making Mistakes

V + (으)ㄴ 적이 있다 — "have (ever) done"

Form consonant stem + 은 적이 있다/없다 · vowel stem + ㄴ 적이 있다/없다

Talks about past experience — whether you have or haven't ever done something.

한국에서 길을 잃은 적이 있어요.

I've gotten lost in Korea before.

제주도에 가 본 적이 없어요.

I've never been to Jeju Island.

A/V + 는 편이다 — "tend to / be rather"

Form Verb + 는 편이다 · Adjective + -(으)ㄴ 편이다

Says something is generally the case or leans a certain way, without being absolute.

저는 실수를 자주 하는 편이에요.

I tend to make mistakes often.

이 동네는 조용한 편이에요.

This neighborhood is rather quiet.

Form consonant stem + 으려고 하다 · vowel stem + 려고 하다

Expresses an intention or plan to do something (often soon).

소포를 보내려고 해요.

UNIT 16행사, 축제Events & Festivals

V + (으)ㄹ + N — "the N that will … (future modifier)"

Form consonant stem + 을 + N · vowel stem + ㄹ + N (often with 사람, 계획, 생각)

A verb clause describing a noun in the future — used for plans and intentions.

행사에 참가할 사람 있어요?

Is there anyone who will join the event?

다음 달에 이사할 생각이에요.

I'm thinking of moving next month.

A/V + 거든요 — "(it's) because… (explaining)"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 거든요 (spoken)

Gives a reason or background the listener doesn't yet know, usually answering a question.

엘리베이터를 점검해서 이용할 수 없거든요.

It's because the elevator is under inspection, so it can't be used.

오늘 일찍 일어났거든요. 그래서 피곤해요.

I woke up early today, you see — that's why I'm tired.
