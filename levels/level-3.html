UNIT 1 대인 관계 Relationship Worries & Advice

-고 해서 — "(because…) and so, for those reasons"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 고 해서 (often A-고 B-고 해서)

Gives one representative reason out of several implied ones — "(partly) because…, and so." Doubling it (…-고 …-고 해서) lists two or more reasons explicitly. The result clause cannot be a command or suggestion.

공감대도 없고 바쁘고 해서 친해지기가 어려워요.

We have little in common and we're both busy, so it's hard to get close.

일하는 방법도 모르고 해서 직장 생활이 힘들어요.

I don't really know how things are done, so work life is tough.

월급도 적고 일도 많고 해서 회사를 그만뒀어요.

The pay was low and there was a lot of work, so (for those reasons) I quit the company.

날씨도 춥고 해서 오늘은 그냥 집에 있었어요.

It was cold and so on, so today I just stayed home.

Tip The result clause states a plain fact or past outcome — it can't be an order or suggestion. Say 그만뒀어요 ("I quit"), not 그만두세요 ("quit it"). For "so let's…", use -(으)니까 instead.

-(으)면 되다 — "just need to…, all you have to do is"

Form Verb stem + -(으)면 되다  ·  negative: -지 않으면 되다 / 안 -(으)면 되다

Offers a simple solution or sufficient condition: doing this much is enough. Common when giving easy advice or reassurance. Verb stem + -(으)면 되다.

웃으면서 먼저 인사하면 돼요.

You just need to smile and greet them first.

직접 가서 이야기하면 돼요.

All you have to do is go and talk to them in person.

문제도 많이 풀어 보고 단어도 많이 외우면 돼요.

You just need to do lots of practice problems and memorize plenty of vocabulary.

서류는 이메일로 보내 주시면 됩니다.

You just need to send the documents by email.

Compare -(으)면 되다 = "this much is enough"; -아/어야 되다 = "this is required, you must." 가면 돼요 ("you may just go") vs. 가야 돼요 ("you have to go").

UNIT 2 성격 Describing Personality

-아/어지다 — "to become / get (gradually)"

Form Adjective stem + 아/어지다 (ㅏ/ㅗ → 아지다 · others → 어지다 · 하다 → 해지다)

Turns an adjective into a verb of change: the subject gradually comes to have that quality (ㅏ/ㅗ → 아지다 · others → 어지다 · 하다 → 해지다). Used for changes in personality, mood, weather, condition, etc.

예전에는 실수를 자주 했는데 요즘은 꼼꼼해졌어요.

I used to make a lot of mistakes, but these days I've become meticulous. (꼼꼼하다 → 해지다)

태권도를 배운 후부터 아이가 활발해졌어요.

Ever since taking taekwondo, my child has become more outgoing.

그는 성격이 많이 밝아졌어요.

His personality has become much brighter. (밝다 has ㅏ → 아지다)

취직을 한 후로 표정이 한결 밝아지고 자신감도 생겼어요.

Since getting a job, his expression has grown much brighter and he's gained confidence too.

-는 대신(에) — "instead of / in return for"

Form Verb + 는 대신에 · Adj + -(으)ㄴ 대신에 · Noun + 대신에

Two uses: (1) replacing one action with another ("instead of"), and (2) compensating — one downside is balanced by an upside ("but on the other hand"). Verb + 는 대신에, Adjective + -(으)ㄴ 대신에.

일요일에 일하는 대신에 월요일에 쉬어요.

Instead of working on Sunday, I rest on Monday.

이 일은 시간이 오래 걸리는 대신에 실수가 없어요.

This work takes a long time, but in return there are no mistakes.

밖에서 외식하는 대신에 집에서 직접 요리해서 돈을 아꼈어요.

Instead of eating out, I cooked at home myself and saved money.

이 식당은 값이 조금 비싼 대신에 재료가 신선하고 양도 많아요.

This restaurant is a bit pricey, but in return the ingredients are fresh and the portions are big. (비싸다 is an adjective → 비싼 대신에)

UNIT 3 지역 복지 서비스 Using Welfare Services

-는지 알다 / 모르다 — "to know / not know whether…"

Form Verb + 는지 · Adj + -(으)ㄴ지 · past + -았/었는지, then 알다 / 모르다

Embeds a question (whether / what / when / how…) as the object of 알다 (know) or 모르다 (not know). Verb + 는지, Adjective + -(으)ㄴ지. Frequently pairs with question words 어디, 언제, 어떻게, 무엇.

외국인도 복지 서비스를 이용할 수 있는지 아는 사람이 많지 않아요.

Not many people know whether foreigners can also use welfare services.

상담 센터가 몇 시에 문을 여는지 몰라서 홈페이지를 찾아봤어요.

I didn't know what time the counseling center opens, so I checked the website.

이 서류를 어디에 제출해야 하는지 아세요?

Do you know where I'm supposed to submit these documents?

신청 방법이 얼마나 복잡한지 몰라서 직접 가서 물어봤어요.

I didn't know how complicated the application process was, so I went and asked in person. (복잡하다 is an adjective → 복잡한지)

-다가 — "while doing…, then (interrupted / switched)"

Form Verb stem + 다가  ·  same subject in both clauses

One action is in progress when it stops and a second action takes over, or when something else happens. Implies the first action (Verb stem + 다가) is interrupted or changes course midway. Both clauses share the same subject.

복지관에 가다가 친구를 만났어요.

On my way to the welfare center, I ran into a friend.

영화를 보다가 재미없어서 나왔어요.

I was watching a movie but left because it was boring.

책을 읽다가 친구가 와서 밖에 나갔어요.

I was reading a book, then a friend came so I went out.

텔레비전을 보다가 깜빡 잠이 들었어요.

I was watching TV, and then I dozed off without realizing it.

Compare -다가 interrupts an ongoing action (가다가 = "while on the way"); -고 나서 waits until the first action fully finishes (가고 나서 = "after going"). Adding past tense — -았/었다가 — means the first action completed and then reversed: 갔다가 왔어요 ("I went and came back").

UNIT 4 교환과 환불 Product Exchange & Refund

-(으)ㄹ 만하다 — "worth doing / good enough to"

Form Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 만하다

Says an action is worth the effort, or that something is acceptable / bearable. Two shades: a recommendation ("worth trying") and tolerance ("not bad, manageable"). Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 만하다.

물냉면은 맵지 않아서 먹을 만해요.

Cold noodles aren't spicy, so they're worth trying. (먹다 is consonant-final → 먹을 만해요)

요즘 볼 만한 영화가 있으면 소개해 주세요.

If there's a movie worth watching these days, please recommend one. (보다 is vowel-final → 볼 만한)

이 가게는 가격도 적당하고 직원도 친절해서 한번 가 볼 만해요.

This shop has reasonable prices and friendly staff, so it's worth a visit.

방이 조금 좁기는 하지만 혼자 살기에는 지낼 만해요.

The room is a little small, but it's manageable enough to live in alone.

-아/어 가지고 — "because… and then, so (spoken)"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 아/어 가지고  ·  colloquial

A conversational connector linking a cause/situation to its result, or one action to a following one (ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아 가지고 · others + 어 가지고). Similar to -아/어서 but more casual and spoken.

얼룩이 있어 가지고 환불했어요.

There was a stain, so I got a refund.

새 자동차는 너무 비싸 가지고 지금은 못 사요.

The new car is too expensive, so I can't buy it right now.

어제 늦게 자 가지고 오늘 너무 피곤해요.

I went to bed late yesterday, so I'm really tired today.

버스를 잘못 타 가지고 약속에 늦었어요.

I got on the wrong bus, and so I was late for the appointment.

Note Mainly spoken; in writing prefer -아/어서. The first and second clauses share the same subject.

UNIT 5 소비와 절약 Spending & Saving Money

N + 이나 / 밖에 — "as much as… / only, no more than"

Form N + 이나 (a lot — positive verb) · N + 밖에 + negative verb (only a little)

A contrasting pair for quantities. 이나 expresses surprise that an amount is large; 밖에 (always with a negative ending) stresses that an amount is small / insufficient.

이번 달에 교통비로 12만 원이나 썼어요?

You spent as much as 120,000 won on transport this month?

산 지 1년밖에 안 됐는데 벌써 고장 났어요.

It's only been a year since I bought it, and it's already broken. (밖에 always takes a negative — 안 됐다)

친구는 주말에 영화를 세 편이나 봤대요.

My friend says she watched as many as three movies over the weekend.

월급을 받았는데 통장에 10만 원밖에 안 남았어요.

I got paid, but only 100,000 won is left in my account.

Tip 밖에 must pair with a negative verb (안/못/없다), but it already means "only" — don't add 만. Say 한 명밖에 없어요, not 한 명만밖에 없어요.

-(ㄴ/는)다고 하다 — "(it is said / they say) that…"

Form reported statement, by type:

Original Reported form
Verb (present) -(느)ㄴ다고 하다 — 간다고 / 먹는다고
Adjective -다고 하다 — 싸다고 / 좋다고
Noun + 이다 -(이)라고 하다 — 가계부라고
Past -았/었다고 하다 — 샀다고

Reports someone's statement indirectly. The quoted clause takes plain (반말) endings before 고 하다. Often shortened in speech to -대요 (싼대요, 간대요).

중고 매장에 가면 싸게 살 수 있다고 해요.

They say you can buy things cheaply at secondhand stores.

그는 하루에 커피 두 잔밖에 안 마신다고 해요.

He says he drinks only two cups of coffee a day. (verb 마시다 → 마신다고)

일기 예보에서 이번 주말에 비가 많이 온다고 해요.

The weather forecast says it will rain a lot this weekend.

친구가 다음 달에 고향으로 돌아간다고 했어요.

My friend said she's going back to her hometown next month.

Tip The reported clause uses present plain form: verbs add -ㄴ다/는다 (간다고, 먹는다고), but adjectives and 이다 keep the bare -다 (싸다고, 학생이라고). A common error is 싸ㄴ다고 — adjectives never take ㄴ다.

피동 표현 — "passive verbs (be ~ed)"

Form active verb + passive infix -이-, -히-, -리-, -기-

Infix Active → Passive Meaning
-이- 보다 → 보이다 to be seen
-히- 막다 → 막히다 to be blocked
-리- 열다 → 열리다 to be opened
-기- 끊다 → 끊기다 to be cut off

Passive verbs are formed by inserting -이/히/리/기-. The thing affected becomes the subject (이/가); the action happens to it rather than being done by it.

와, 방 안에서 산이 보이네요?

Wow, you can see the mountains from inside the room? (보다 → 보이다)

출근 시간이라서 길이 많이 막혔어요.

It was rush hour, so the roads were badly jammed. (막다 → 막히다)

친구와 통화 중에 전화가 끊겼어요.

The call got cut off while I was talking to my friend. (끊다 → 끊기다)

바람이 불어서 갑자기 문이 닫혔어요.

The wind blew, and the door suddenly closed. (닫다 → 닫히다)

Note There is no rule for which infix a verb takes — each is memorized. Many verbs use -아/어지다 instead (만들어지다, 켜지다), and a few have no passive at all and rely on 되다 (사용되다). When unsure, learn the passive as its own word.

-자고 하다 — "(someone) suggests / suggested that…"

Form Verb stem + 자고 하다 (reported suggestion; negative: -지 말자고 하다)

Reports a suggestion or proposal someone made (the direct form being -(으)ㅂ시다 / -자). Verb stem + 자고 하다; shortened in speech to -재요.

친구가 만나자고 해서 약속 장소에 나갔어요.

아내가 같이 저녁을 먹자고 했어요.

My wife suggested having dinner together.

동료들이 주말에 같이 등산을 가자고 했어요.

My colleagues suggested going hiking together this weekend.

사동 표현 — "causative verbs (make / let ~ do)"

Form verb + causative infix -이-, -히-, -리-, -기-, -우-, -추-

Base → Causative Meaning
먹다 → 먹이다 to feed (make eat)
입다 → 입히다 to dress (someone)
울다 → 울리다 to make (someone) cry
끓다 → 끓이다 to boil (something)
타다 → 태우다 to give a ride
낮다 → 낮추다 to lower (something)

Causative verbs mean the subject makes or lets someone/something do the action, formed with infixes -이/히/리/기/우/추-. Many cooking verbs are causative (끓이다 to boil, 익히다 to cook through, 식히다 to cool).

성민은 토끼에게 당근을 먹이고 있어요.

Seongmin is feeding carrots to the rabbit. (먹다 eat → 먹이다 feed)

물을 끓여서 면을 넣으세요.

Boil the water and add the noodles. (끓다 boils → 끓이다 boil it)

우는 아이를 달래려고 재미있는 노래를 불러서 웃겼어요.

To soothe the crying child, I sang a funny song and made them laugh. (웃다 laugh → 웃기다 make laugh)

아기가 추울까 봐 두꺼운 옷을 입혔어요.

I dressed the baby in thick clothes in case he got cold. (입다 wear → 입히다 dress someone)

Compare Some stems take an infix for both voices but with different ones: 보다 → 보이다 can mean both passive "be seen" and causative "show." Context and the object (이/가 vs. 을/를) tell them apart. For people, -게 하다 (Unit 13) is the productive way to say "make someone do."

Offers a tentative, uncertain reason for an observed result — "perhaps it's because…" (Verb / Adjective stem + 아/어서 그런지). The speaker is guessing, not stating a definite cause.

더운 나라에서 와서 그런지 한국 겨울이 너무 추워요.

노트북이 오래돼서 그런지 요즘 자꾸 느려져요.

Maybe because the laptop is old, it keeps getting slow these days.

주말이라서 그런지 식당에 사람이 정말 많네요.

요즘 잠을 잘 못 자서 그런지 자꾸 피곤하고 집중이 안 돼요.

A gentler, more polite way to ask than a plain -아요/어요? question. Verb + 나요?, Adjective + -(으)ㄴ가요?. It softens the tone — useful when asking carefully, asking a stranger, or seeking confirmation politely.

예방 주사를 맞을 때 많이 아팠나요?

-기 위해(서) — "in order to / for the purpose of"

States the goal or purpose of an action: Verb stem + 기 위해(서), Noun + 을/를 위해(서). More formal than -(으)려고; common in writing, résumés, and announcements.

한국 국적을 취득하기 위해 1년 전부터 한국어를 공부하고 있다.

I've been studying Korean for a year in order to obtain Korean citizenship.

Form ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아 놓다 · other stems + 어 놓다 · 하다 → 해 놓다

An action is finished ahead of time and its result is kept ready for later (ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아 놓다 · others + 어 놓다 · 하다 → 해 놓다). Close in meaning to -아/어 두다.

면접을 준비하기 위해 자기소개서를 써 놓았어요.

I wrote my cover letter in advance to prepare for the interview.

숙제를 미리 해 놓아서 마음이 편해요.

I did my homework ahead of time, so I feel at ease.

손님이 오기 전에 음식을 미리 만들어 놓았어요.

I made the food in advance before the guests arrived.

중요한 회의가 있어서 알람을 여러 개 맞춰 놓았어요.

I have an important meeting, so I set several alarms ahead of time.

방이 좁은 데다가 어두워서 별로 마음에 안 들어요.

The room is small and, on top of that, dark, so I don't really like it.

He's good at his job and, on top of that, has a nice personality, so everyone likes him. (verb 잘하다 → 잘하는 데다가)

-(ㄴ/는)다 — "plain (declarative) form, for writing"

The neutral written style used for facts, reports, news, diaries, and notices — no listener is addressed. Verbs take -ㄴ다/는다; adjectives and 이다 keep the dictionary -다.

Even when I don't have time, I always eat breakfast.

No matter how sick I am, I never miss class. (아프다 → 아파도)

No matter how busy I am, I always make time to spend with my family.

Even if it's a bit expensive, I'll buy it if the quality is good.

UNIT 13 직장 생활 Work Culture

-게 하다 — "make / let (someone) do"

Form Verb stem + 게 하다

Causative construction: the subject makes or allows another person to do the action (Verb stem + 게 하다). The person made to act is usually marked with 에게/한테 or 이/가.

아이에게 자기 방을 스스로 정리하게 하세요.

Make your child tidy their own room by themselves.

상사가 저를 일찍 퇴근하게 했어요.

My boss let me leave work early.

출입국·외국인청에서 외국인 등록을 하게 합니다.

The immigration office requires foreigners to register.

선생님은 학생들에게 매일 일기를 쓰게 했어요.

The teacher had the students write a diary every day.

Compare -게 하다 works with any verb and is the general way to say "make someone do." The infix causatives of Unit 8 (먹이다, 입히다) only exist for certain verbs and often imply more hands-on action — 옷을 입혀요 ("dress them yourself") vs. 옷을 입게 해요 ("have them get dressed").

-아/어 가다 — "gradually / go on (doing)"

Form Verb stem + 아/어 가다 (vs. -아/어 오다 = up to now)

Describes an action or change progressing onward into the future from the present moment (Verb stem + 아/어 가다). The pair -아/어 오다 means the process has continued up until now.

한국에 온 지 거의 3년이 되어 가요.

It's been almost three years since I came to Korea.

지금은 한국 사람들의 일하는 방식을 알아 가는 중이에요.

I'm gradually getting to know how Koreans work.

처음에는 서툴렀지만 일에 조금씩 익숙해져 가고 있어요.

At first I was clumsy, but little by little I'm getting used to the job.

준비하던 시험이 이제 거의 끝나 가서 마음이 한결 가벼워요.

The exam I'd been preparing for is nearly over now, so I feel much lighter.

Compare -아/어 가다 looks forward from now (살아 가다 = "go on living"); -아/어 오다 looks back to now (살아 오다 = "have lived up to now"). Pick by the direction in time the change is heading.

-잖아(요) — "you know…, as you're aware"

Form Verb / Adjective / N-이다 stem + 잖아요 (casual: 잖아)

Reminds the listener of something they already know, or gently points out an obvious fact (Verb / Adjective / N-이다 stem + 잖아요). Not used for brand-new information, since it appeals to shared knowledge.

로그인을 해야 이용할 수 있잖아요.

You know you have to log in to use it.

수지는 항상 열심히 공부하잖아요.

You know Sooji always studies hard.

영상 통화가 있잖아요. 부모님께 자주 연락드리세요.

There's video calling, you know — contact your parents often.

The traffic will be bad today. It's rush hour right now, you know. (noun 시간 + 이잖아요)

여권이 있어야 비행기를 탈 수 있다.

You must have a passport to board a plane.

You have to leave now so you won't be late.

Bread is only good when it's soft. (ㅂ-irregular 부드럽다 → 부드러워야)

-(으)려던 참이다 — "was just about to…"

Expresses that the speaker was on the very point of doing something just as another event occurred (Verb stem + -(으)려던 참이다). Conveys good timing or coincidence.

친구에게 사과를 하려던 참이었는데 친구가 먼저 미안하다고 했어요.

I was just about to apologize when my friend said sorry first.

지갑을 사려던 참이었는데 친구가 선물해 줬어요.

I was just about to buy a wallet when my friend gave me one as a gift.

막 점심을 먹으려던 참이었는데 갑자기 회의가 잡혔어요.

I was just about to have lunch when a meeting was suddenly scheduled. (먹다 consonant-final → 먹으려던)

안 그래도 너한테 전화하려던 참이었어. 잘 연락했다.

I was actually just about to call you. Good thing you got in touch.

-자마자 — "as soon as, right after"

Form Verb stem + 자마자 (no tense on the first verb)

The second action follows immediately after the first finishes (Verb stem + 자마자). Tense is marked only on the final verb.

집에 돌아가자마자 자요.

As soon as I get home, I sleep.

어머니 목소리를 듣자마자 눈물이 났어요.

The moment I heard my mother's voice, tears came.

소식을 듣자마자 친구에게 전화를 걸었어요.

As soon as I heard the news, I called my friend.

회사에 도착하자마자 갑자기 비가 쏟아지기 시작했어요.

Right after I arrived at the office, it suddenly started pouring.

Note Never add tense to the first verb — say 도착하자마자, not 도착했자마자, even when the whole sentence is past. Unlike 후에, 자마자 stresses that the two events happen back-to-back with no gap.

-(으)ㄹ 텐데 — "it must / probably… so"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)ㄹ 텐데 (past: -았/었을 텐데)

Expresses a strong supposition or assumption (Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)ㄹ 텐데), usually followed by a related suggestion, request, or concern.

밖에 비가 오고 있을 텐데 우산 가져가세요.

It's probably raining outside, so take an umbrella.

바쁘실 텐데 시간을 내 주셔서 감사합니다.

You must be busy, so thank you for making time.

친구가 기다리고 있을 텐데 빨리 가야겠어요.

My friend is probably waiting, so I should hurry.

하루 종일 일하셨으면 많이 피곤하실 텐데 좀 쉬세요.

You must be very tired after working all day, so please get some rest.

-아/어 있다 — "to be in a state (after an action)"

Form ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아 있다 · other stems + 어 있다

Describes the continuing state that remains after an action is completed (ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아 있다 · others + 어 있다) — distinct from -고 있다 (action in progress). Common with intransitive verbs (앉다, 서다, 열리다, 꺼지다).

창문이 열려 있어요.

The window is (standing) open.

전원이 꺼져 있어요.

The power is turned off.

이 커피에는 설탕이 안 들어 있어요.

This coffee has no sugar in it.

할머니가 공원 벤치에 혼자 앉아 있어요.

An old woman is sitting alone on a park bench.

Compare 앉고 있어요 = is in the act of sitting down · 앉 아 있어요 = is (already) seated. Use -아/어 있다 with intransitive verbs only; transitive verbs take -아/어 놓다/두다 for a kept result (문을 열어 놓았어요).
