KIIP Level 3 — Grammar & Vocabulary

A self-study reference for 한국어와 한국문화 중급 1 (current 16-unit edition) · Korea Immigration & Integration Program

This reference follows the current KIIP Level 3 textbook (한국어와 한국문화 중급 1), organized into its 16 units. Each unit introduces its real-life topic, the grammar patterns taught in that unit — with form, meaning, attachment rules, usage notes, and worked examples — and the vocabulary theme that goes with it. A grammar index and a conjugation appendix follow at the end.

How to read each entry

V
Verb (동사) — e.g. 가다, 먹다
A
Adjective / descriptive verb (형용사) — e.g. 크다, 예쁘다
N
Noun (명사) — e.g. 사람, 학교
-(으)
Use -으 after a consonant-final stem, drop it after a vowel-final stem
아 / 어
Choose 아 after ㅏ/ㅗ stem vowels, otherwise 어 (하다 → 해)

UNIT 1 대인 관계 Relationship Worries & Advice

-고 해서 — "(because…) and so, for those reasons"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 고 해서 (often A-고 B-고 해서)

Gives one representative reason out of several implied ones — "(partly) because…, and so." Doubling it (…-고 …-고 해서) lists two or more reasons explicitly. The result clause cannot be a command or suggestion.

공감대도 없고 바쁘고 해서 친해지기가 어려워요.

We have little in common and we're both busy, so it's hard to get close.

일하는 방법도 모르고 해서 직장 생활이 힘들어요.

I don't really know how things are done, so work life is tough.

-(으)면 되다 — "just need to…, all you have to do is"

Form Verb stem + -(으)면 되다  ·  negative: -지 않으면 되다 / 안 -(으)면 되다

Offers a simple solution or sufficient condition: doing this much is enough. Common when giving easy advice or reassurance.

웃으면서 먼저 인사하면 돼요.

You just need to smile and greet them first.

직접 가서 이야기하면 돼요.

All you have to do is go and talk to them in person.

문제도 많이 풀어 보고 단어도 많이 외우면 돼요.

You just need to do lots of practice problems and memorize plenty of vocabulary.

UNIT 2 성격 Describing Personality

-아/어지다 — "to become / get (gradually)"

Form Adjective stem + 아/어지다 (ㅏ/ㅗ → 아지다 · others → 어지다 · 하다 → 해지다)

Turns an adjective into a verb of change: the subject gradually comes to have that quality. Used for changes in personality, mood, weather, condition, etc.

예전에는 실수를 자주 했는데 요즘은 꼼꼼해졌어요.

I used to make a lot of mistakes, but these days I've become meticulous.

태권도를 배운 후부터 아이가 활발해졌어요.

Ever since taking taekwondo, my child has become more outgoing.

그는 성격이 많이 밝아졌어요.

His personality has become much brighter.

-는 대신(에) — "instead of / in return for"

Form Verb + 는 대신에 · Adj + -(으)ㄴ 대신에 · Noun + 대신에

Two uses: (1) replacing one action with another ("instead of"), and (2) compensating — one downside is balanced by an upside ("but on the other hand").

일요일에 일하는 대신에 월요일에 쉬어요.

Instead of working on Sunday, I rest on Monday.

이 일은 시간이 오래 걸리는 대신에 실수가 없어요.

This work takes a long time, but in return there are no mistakes.

UNIT 3 지역 복지 서비스 Using Welfare Services

-는지 알다 / 모르다 — "to know / not know whether…"

Form Verb + 는지 · Adj + -(으)ㄴ지 · past + -았/었는지, then 알다 / 모르다

Embeds a question (whether / what / when / how…) as the object of 알다 (know) or 모르다 (not know). Frequently pairs with question words 어디, 언제, 어떻게, 무엇.

외국인도 복지 서비스를 이용할 수 있는지 아는 사람이 많지 않아요.

Not many people know whether foreigners can also use welfare services.

상담 센터가 몇 시에 문을 여는지 몰라서 홈페이지를 찾아봤어요.

I didn't know what time the counseling center opens, so I checked the website.

-다가 — "while doing…, then (interrupted / switched)"

Form Verb stem + 다가  ·  same subject in both clauses

One action is in progress when it stops and a second action takes over, or when something else happens. Implies the first action is interrupted or changes course midway.

복지관에 가다가 친구를 만났어요.

On my way to the welfare center, I ran into a friend.

영화를 보다가 재미없어서 나왔어요.

I was watching a movie but left because it was boring.

책을 읽다가 친구가 와서 밖에 나갔어요.

I was reading a book, then a friend came so I went out.

UNIT 4 교환과 환불 Product Exchange & Refund

-(으)ㄹ 만하다 — "worth doing / good enough to"

Form Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 만하다

Says an action is worth the effort, or that something is acceptable / bearable. Two shades: a recommendation ("worth trying") and tolerance ("not bad, manageable").

물냉면은 맵지 않아서 먹을 만해요.

Cold noodles aren't spicy, so they're worth trying.

요즘 볼 만한 영화가 있으면 소개해 주세요.

If there's a movie worth watching these days, please recommend one.

-아/어 가지고 — "because… and then, so (spoken)"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 아/어 가지고  ·  colloquial

A conversational connector linking a cause/situation to its result, or one action to a following one. Similar to -아/어서 but more casual and spoken.

얼룩이 있어 가지고 환불했어요.

There was a stain, so I got a refund.

새 자동차는 비싸 가지고 지금은 못 사겠어요.

The new car is expensive, so I can't buy it right now.

Note Mainly spoken; in writing prefer -아/어서. The first and second clauses share the same subject.

UNIT 5 소비와 절약 Spending & Saving Money

N + 이나 / 밖에 — "as much as… / only, no more than"

Form N + 이나 (a lot — positive verb) · N + 밖에 + negative verb (only a little)

A contrasting pair for quantities. 이나 expresses surprise that an amount is large; 밖에 (always with a negative ending) stresses that an amount is small / insufficient.

이번 달에 교통비로 12만 원이나 썼어요?

You spent as much as 120,000 won on transport this month?

산 지 1년밖에 안 됐는데 벌써 고장 났어요.

It's only been a year since I bought it, and it's already broken.

-(ㄴ/는)다고 하다 — "(it is said / they say) that…"

Form reported statement, by type:

Original Reported form
Verb (present) -(느)ㄴ다고 하다 — 간다고 / 먹는다고
Adjective -다고 하다 — 싸다고 / 좋다고
Noun + 이다 -(이)라고 하다 — 가계부라고
Past -았/었다고 하다 — 샀다고

Reports someone's statement indirectly. The quoted clause takes plain (반말) endings before 고 하다. Often shortened in speech to -대요 (싼대요, 간대요).

중고 매장에 가면 싸게 살 수 있다고 해요.

They say you can buy things cheaply at secondhand stores.

그는 하루에 커피 두 잔밖에 안 마신다고 해요.

He says he drinks only two cups of coffee a day.

UNIT 6 주거 환경 Living Environment

피동 표현 — "passive verbs (be ~ed)"

Form active verb + passive infix -이-, -히-, -리-, -기-

Infix Active → Passive Meaning
-이- 보다 → 보이다 to be seen
-히- 막다 → 막히다 to be blocked
-리- 열다 → 열리다 to be opened
-기- 끊다 → 끊기다 to be cut off

Passive verbs are formed by inserting -이/히/리/기-. The thing affected becomes the subject (이/가); the action happens to it rather than being done by it.

와, 방 안에서 산이 보이네요?

Wow, you can see the mountains from inside the room?

친구와 통화 중에 전화가 끊겼어요.

The call got cut off while I was talking to my friend.

-자고 하다 — "(someone) suggests / suggested that…"

Form Verb stem + 자고 하다 (reported suggestion; negative: -지 말자고 하다)

Reports a suggestion or proposal someone made (the direct form being -(으)ㅂ시다 / -자). Shortened in speech to -재요.

친구가 만나자고 해서 약속 장소에 나갔어요.

My friend suggested meeting, so I went to the meeting spot.

아내가 같이 저녁을 먹자고 했어요.

My wife suggested having dinner together.

UNIT 7 문화생활 Cultural Activities

-(으)라고 하다 / -냐고 하다 — "told to… / asked whether…"

Form command: Verb + -(으)라고 하다 · question: V/A + -(느)냐고 하다

Reports a command (the direct form -(으)세요 / -아라) with -(으)라고 하다, and reports a question with -냐고 하다. Spoken short forms: -(으)래요 and -냬요.

직원이 관객들에게 입장하라고 했어요.

The staff told the audience to go in.

공연이 몇 시에 시작하냐고 했어요.

He asked what time the show starts.

N + 만큼 — "as much as / to the extent of"

Form Noun + 만큼 · also Verb/Adj + -(으)ㄹ/-는 만큼

Marks a standard of comparison — something is equal in degree to the noun. Often used to say two things are similar in amount, quality, or extent.

회사 앞 식당은 고향 음식만큼 맛있어요.

The restaurant near work is as tasty as home cooking.

공연이 기대만큼 재미있었어요.

The show was as fun as I expected.

평일은 주말만큼 사람이 많지 않아요.

On weekdays there aren't as many people as on weekends.

UNIT 8 요리 방법 Cooking & Recipes

사동 표현 — "causative verbs (make / let ~ do)"

Form verb + causative infix -이-, -히-, -리-, -기-, -우-, -추-

Base → Causative Meaning
먹다 → 먹이다 to feed (make eat)
입다 → 입히다 to dress (someone)
울다 → 울리다 to make (someone) cry
끓다 → 끓이다 to boil (something)
타다 → 태우다 to give a ride
낮다 → 낮추다 to lower (something)

Causative verbs mean the subject makes or lets someone/something do the action. Many cooking verbs are causative (끓이다 to boil, 익히다 to cook through, 식히다 to cool).

성민은 토끼에게 당근을 먹이고 있어요.

Seongmin is feeding carrots to the rabbit.

물을 끓여서 면을 넣으세요.

Boil the water and add the noodles.

음식이 맛있어서 남기지 않고 다 먹었어요.

The food was delicious, so I ate it all without leaving any.

UNIT 9 고장과 수리 Breakdowns & Repairs

-아/어서 그런지 — "maybe because…"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 아/어서 그런지

Offers a tentative, uncertain reason for an observed result — "perhaps it's because…". The speaker is guessing, not stating a definite cause.

더운 나라에서 와서 그런지 한국 겨울이 너무 추워요.

Maybe because I'm from a hot country, Korean winters feel too cold.

전원이 안 켜져서 그런지 화면이 안 나와요.

Maybe because the power won't turn on, the screen isn't showing anything.

-나요? / -(으)ㄴ가요? — "softened, polite question"

Form Verb + 나요? · Adjective + -(으)ㄴ가요? · N + 인가요?

A gentler way to ask than plain -아요/어요?. It softens the tone — useful when asking carefully, asking a stranger, or seeking confirmation politely.

어디가 고장 났나요?

What part is broken?

예방 주사를 맞을 때 많이 아팠나요?

Did the vaccination hurt a lot?

수리비가 많이 비싼가요?

Is the repair cost quite expensive?

UNIT 10 취업과 이력서 Job Search & Résumé

-기 위해(서) — "in order to / for the purpose of"

Form Verb stem + 기 위해(서) · Noun + 을/를 위해(서)

States the goal or purpose of an action. More formal than -(으)려고; common in writing, résumés, and announcements.

한국 국적을 취득하기 위해 1년 전부터 한국어를 공부하고 있다.

I've been studying Korean for a year in order to obtain Korean citizenship.

일하는 엄마들은 아이를 맡길 곳을 찾기 위해 노력하고 있다.

Working mothers are striving to find places to leave their children.

-아/어 놓다 — "do in advance and keep (the result)"

Form ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아 놓다 · other stems + 어 놓다 · 하다 → 해 놓다

An action is finished ahead of time and its result is kept ready for later. Close in meaning to -아/어 두다.

면접을 준비하기 위해 자기소개서를 써 놓았어요.

I wrote my cover letter in advance to prepare for the interview.

숙제를 미리 해 놓아서 마음이 편해요.

I did my homework ahead of time, so I feel at ease.

UNIT 11 부동산 (집 구하기) House Hunting

-(으)ㄴ/는 데다(가) — "on top of / moreover"

Form Verb + 는 데다가 · Adj + -(으)ㄴ 데다가

Adds a second fact in the same direction as the first — they pile up. Both clauses are positive together, or both negative together.

이 집은 교통이 편리한 데다가 주변에 편의시설도 많아요.

This place has convenient transport, and on top of that, lots of facilities nearby.

방이 좁은 데다가 어두워서 별로 마음에 안 들어요.

The room is small and, on top of that, dark, so I don't really like it.

-(ㄴ/는)다 — "plain (declarative) form, for writing"

Form Verb + -(느)ㄴ다 · Adjective + 다 · Noun + (이)다

The neutral written style used for facts, reports, news, diaries, and notices — no listener is addressed. Verbs take -ㄴ다/는다; adjectives and 이다 keep the dictionary -다.

행복 아파트는 교통이 편리하고 다양한 편의시설이 있다.

Happy Apartments have convenient transport and various facilities.

The most common type of housing in Korea is the apartment.

UNIT 12 명절과 풍습 Traditional Holidays

-아/어도 — "even if / even though"

Form ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아도 · other stems + 어도 · 하다 → 해도

Concessive ending: the result holds regardless of the condition. Often strengthened with 아무리 ("no matter how").

나는 시간이 없어도 아침밥을 꼭 먹는다.

Even when I don't have time, I always eat breakfast.

몸이 아무리 아파도 수업에 결석하지 않는다.

No matter how sick I am, I never miss class.

-게 되다 — "come to / end up (doing)"

Form Verb stem + 게 되다

Marks a change of state or a result that comes about through circumstances rather than one's own direct choice — "it turned out that…", "I ended up…".

한국에 살게 되면서 추석을 처음 알게 됐어요.

After I came to live in Korea, I learned about Chuseok for the first time.

장사가 안 돼서 가게 문을 닫게 되었다.

Business was bad, so the shop ended up closing.

이번에 새로운 회사에 들어가게 되어서 정말 기뻐요.

I'm really happy that I've come to join a new company.

UNIT 13 직장 생활 Work Culture

-게 하다 — "make / let (someone) do"

Form Verb stem + 게 하다

Causative construction: the subject makes or allows another person to do the action. The person made to act is usually marked with 에게/한테 or 이/가.

아이에게 자기 방을 스스로 정리하게 하세요.

Make your child tidy their own room by themselves.

상사가 저를 일찍 퇴근하게 했어요.

My boss let me leave work early.

출입국·외국인청에서 외국인 등록을 하게 합니다.

The immigration office requires foreigners to register.

-아/어 가다 — "gradually / go on (doing)"

Form Verb stem + 아/어 가다 (vs. -아/어 오다 = up to now)

Describes an action or change progressing onward into the future from the present moment. The pair -아/어 오다 means the process has continued up until now.

한국에 온 지 거의 3년이 되어 가요.

It's been almost three years since I came to Korea.

지금은 한국 사람들의 일하는 방식을 알아 가는 중이에요.

I'm gradually getting to know how Koreans work.

UNIT 14 스마트폰 활용 Smartphones & the Internet

-잖아(요) — "you know…, as you're aware"

Form Verb / Adjective / N-이다 stem + 잖아요 (casual: 잖아)

Reminds the listener of something they already know, or gently points out an obvious fact. Not used for brand-new information, since it appeals to shared knowledge.

로그인을 해야 이용할 수 있잖아요.

You know you have to log in to use it.

수지는 항상 열심히 공부하잖아요.

You know Sooji always studies hard.

영상 통화가 있잖아요. 부모님께 자주 연락드리세요.

There's video calling, you know — contact your parents often.

-아/어야 — "must / only if (one does)…"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 아/어야 (then main clause)

States a necessary condition: only when the first clause is satisfied does the second become possible. Often expands to -아/어야 하다/되다 ("must do").

여권이 있어야 비행기를 탈 수 있다.

You must have a passport to board a plane.

You have to leave now so you won't be late.

빵은 부드러워야 맛있다.

Bread is only good when it's soft.

UNIT 15 상담 (고민) Seeking Advice

-(으)려던 참이다 — "was just about to…"

Form Verb stem + -(으)려던 참이다 / 참이었다

Expresses that the speaker was on the very point of doing something just as another event occurred. Conveys good timing or coincidence.

친구에게 사과를 하려던 참이었는데 친구가 먼저 미안하다고 했어요.

I was just about to apologize when my friend said sorry first.

I was just about to buy a wallet when my friend gave me one as a gift.

-자마자 — "as soon as, right after"

Form Verb stem + 자마자 (no tense on the first verb)

The second action follows immediately after the first finishes. Tense is marked only on the final verb.

집에 돌아가자마자 자요.

As soon as I get home, I sleep.

어머니 목소리를 듣자마자 눈물이 났어요.

The moment I heard my mother's voice, tears came.

UNIT 16 날씨와 기후 Weather, Climate & Health

-(으)ㄹ 텐데 — "it must / probably… so"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)ㄹ 텐데 (past: -았/었을 텐데)

Expresses a strong supposition or assumption, usually followed by a related suggestion, request, or concern.

밖에 비가 오고 있을 텐데 우산 가져가세요.

It's probably raining outside, so take an umbrella.

바쁘실 텐데 시간을 내 주셔서 감사합니다.

You must be busy, so thank you for making time.

친구가 기다리고 있을 텐데 빨리 가야겠어요.

My friend is probably waiting, so I should hurry.

-아/어 있다 — "to be in a state (after an action)"

Form ㅏ/ㅗ stem + 아 있다 · other stems + 어 있다

Describes the continuing state that remains after an action is completed — distinct from -고 있다 (action in progress). Common with intransitive verbs (앉다, 서다, 열리다, 꺼지다).

창문이 열려 있어요.

The window is (standing) open.

전원이 꺼져 있어요.

The power is turned off.

이 커피에는 설탕이 안 들어 있어요.

This coffee has no sugar in it.

Compare 앉고 있어요 = is in the act of sitting down · 앉아 있어요 = is (already) seated.
