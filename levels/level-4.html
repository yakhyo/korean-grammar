KIIP Level 4 — Grammar & Vocabulary

A self-study reference for 한국어와 한국문화 중급 2 (16-lesson edition) · Korea Immigration & Integration Program

This reference follows the KIIP Level 4 textbook (한국어와 한국문화 중급 2) — the upper-intermediate stage of the Social Integration Program. It is organized into the book's 16 lessons. At this level the topics shift toward society — science, heritage, globalization, the information age, elections, the economy, law, and immigrant life — and the grammar becomes more abstract: degree and emphasis, reported and quoted speech, supposition, concession, and formal written connectors. Each lesson presents its two grammar points (form, meaning, usage notes, examples) and matching vocabulary. A grammar index closes the book.

LESSON 1한국 생활 적응Adapting to Life in Korea

-느라고 — "because of (doing), occupied with"

Form Verb stem + 느라고  ·  Rule same subject; second clause is usually a negative result

The effort or time spent on the first action causes a (usually unfavorable) result in the second — being unable to do something, being tired, missing out. Both clauses share one subject, and the second clause cannot be a command or suggestion. Attach 느라고 to the verb stem.

한국 생활에 적응하느라고 정신이 없었어요.

I was so caught up adjusting to life in Korea that I was overwhelmed.

일하느라고 가족과 보낼 시간이 부족해요.

Being busy with work, I don't have enough time to spend with my family.

서류를 준비하느라고 점심도 제대로 못 먹었어요.

I was so busy preparing the documents that I couldn't even eat a proper lunch.

아이를 돌보느라고 자기 시간을 거의 못 가져요.

Caught up in caring for the kids, she hardly gets any time for herself.

Compare Unlike plain 아서/어서 ("because"), 느라고 stresses that the time/effort spent on one action caused a negative outcome, requires the same subject in both clauses, and never takes past tense on the stem (적응했느라고 ✗).

-(으)ㄹ수록 — "the more… the more…"

Form consonant stem + 을수록 · vowel/ㄹ stem + ㄹ수록  ·  often 으면 + -(으)ㄹ수록

As the degree of the first situation increases, the second changes accordingly. Frequently reinforced by a leading 으면: 살면 살수록 ("the longer one lives…").

한국어는 공부할수록 재미있어요.

The more I study Korean, the more fun it gets.

한국에 살면 살수록 정이 들어요.

The longer I live in Korea, the more attached I become. (살 ends in ㄹ → ㄹ수록)

값이 쌀수록 품질을 잘 살펴봐야 해요.

The cheaper it is, the more carefully you should check the quality.

사람은 나이가 들수록 건강의 소중함을 깨닫게 돼요.

The older people get, the more they come to realize the value of health.

LESSON 2가족의 변화Changes in the Family

-(으)ㄹ 뿐만 아니라 — "not only… but also"

Form V/A + -(으)ㄹ 뿐만 아니라 · N + 뿐만 아니라 (past: -았/었을 뿐만 아니라)

Adds a second, often stronger point to the first. "Not only X, but also Y." Works with nouns directly (N뿐만 아니라).

요즘은 가족 형태가 다양할 뿐만 아니라 계속 변하고 있어요.

These days family forms are not only diverse but also constantly changing.

그분은 한국어뿐만 아니라 영어도 잘해요.

He is good not only at Korean but also at English. (noun + 뿐만 아니라, no -(으)ㄹ)

이 일은 시간이 많이 걸릴 뿐만 아니라 비용도 많이 들어요.

This task not only takes a lot of time but also costs a lot.

출산율이 낮아질 뿐만 아니라 고령화도 빠르게 진행되고 있어요.

Not only is the birth rate falling, but the aging of society is also progressing rapidly.

-(으)ㄹ 수밖에 없다 — "have no choice but to / be bound to"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)ㄹ 수밖에 없다

There is no other option (with verbs), or an outcome is only natural and unavoidable (with adjectives). Attach -(으)ㄹ 수밖에 없다 to the stem.

맞벌이 가정이 늘면서 보육 시설이 늘어날 수밖에 없어요.

As dual-income households increase, childcare facilities are bound to grow.

버스가 끊겨서 택시를 탈 수밖에 없었어요.

The buses had stopped, so I had no choice but to take a taxi.

물가가 오르니 생활이 힘들 수밖에 없지요.

With prices rising, life is inevitably hard. (힘들 ends in ㄹ → drop 으)

증거가 이렇게 명확하니 그의 말을 믿을 수밖에 없어요.

With the evidence this clear, I have no choice but to believe what he says. (믿 ends in ㄷ → 을)

LESSON 3생활 속의 과학Science in Everyday Life

-(으)ㄴ/는 줄 알다 / 모르다 — "to (mistakenly) think / not know that"

Form V/A modifier + 줄 알다(모르다): 는 줄 (V present) · -(으)ㄴ 줄 (A / V past) · -(으)ㄹ 줄 (future)

Expresses what one assumed, knew, or failed to realize about a fact — often when the assumption turns out wrong. Pairs the modifier form with 줄 알다 / 모르다. (Distinct from -(으)ㄹ 줄 알다 = "to know how to do".)

저는 그 기계가 고장 난 줄 몰랐어요.

I didn't know that machine was broken. (난 = past modifier of 나다)

오늘 회의가 있는 줄 알았는데 아니었어요.

I thought there was a meeting today, but there wasn't.

이 약이 그렇게 효과가 좋은 줄 몰랐어요.

I had no idea this medicine was so effective. (adjective 좋다 → -은 줄)

이 기능이 자동으로 저장되는 줄 알고 따로 저장하지 않았어요.

I thought this feature saved automatically, so I didn't save it separately.

Compare Don't confuse the two senses of -(으)ㄹ 줄 알다: 한국어를 할 줄 알아요 = "I know how to speak Korean" (skill), while 내일 비가 올 줄 알았어요 = "I assumed it would rain" (expectation).

-곤 하다 — "used to / tend to (repeatedly) do"

Form Verb stem + 곤 하다 (past habit: 곤 했다)

Describes a recurring or habitual action. Attach 곤 하다 to the verb stem; in the past tense (곤 했다) it means "used to (do) from time to time".

스마트폰이 없던 시절에는 편지를 쓰곤 했어요.

Back when there were no smartphones, I used to write letters.

스트레스를 받으면 단 음식을 찾곤 해요.

When I'm stressed, I tend to reach for sweet food.

어렸을 때 할머니 댁에 자주 가곤 했어요.

When I was young, I would often go to my grandmother's house.

머리가 복잡할 때면 혼자 강가를 걷곤 해요.

Whenever my mind is in a tangle, I tend to walk along the river alone.

Tip 곤 하다 attaches only to action verbs and needs a repeatable event — not a one-time action. For a single past habit that simply stopped, plain 았/었다 or 던 is more natural.

LESSON 4한국의 의례Korean Ceremonies & Rites

-더니 — "(I observed)… and then / so now"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 더니 (recalls something witnessed; 2nd/3rd person)

Recalls something the speaker personally observed in the past, then states a related result or contrasting change. Attach 더니 to the stem. The subject is usually someone other than the speaker.

친구가 열심히 준비하더니 결국 시험에 합격했어요.

My friend prepared hard, and (sure enough) passed the exam.

아침에는 춥더니 지금은 따뜻해졌어요.

It was cold this morning, but now it's gotten warm.

동생이 한국 드라마를 보더니 한국어에 관심이 생겼어요.

My younger sibling watched Korean dramas and then took an interest in Korean.

그 가게가 처음에는 한산하더니 이제는 손님이 줄을 서요.

That shop was quiet at first, but now customers line up.

Compare 더니 recalls something witnessed (2nd/3rd person), whereas 았/었더니 reports the speaker's own past action and its discovery — see the L7 entry.

-(으)ㄴ 나머지 — "as a result of (extreme)…"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)ㄴ 나머지 (the first state is intense/excessive)

Because of an extreme degree of emotion or state in the first clause, a (usually negative or unexpected) result follows. Attach the -(으)ㄴ 나머지 modifier to the stem.

너무 긴장한 나머지 인사말을 잊어버렸어요.

I was so nervous that I forgot my greeting.

기쁜 나머지 눈물이 났어요.

I was so happy that I cried.

서두른 나머지 중요한 서류를 두고 왔어요.

In my haste, I left an important document behind.

-(으)ㄹ 뻔하다 — "almost / nearly (happened)"

Form Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 뻔하다 (always past: 뻔했다)

Something nearly happened but didn't — usually a narrowly avoided negative event. Attach -(으)ㄹ 뻔하다 to the verb stem, always in the past (뻔했다), often with 하마터면 ("almost / nearly").

빙판길에서 넘어질 뻔했어요.

I almost slipped on the icy road.

하마터면 큰 사고가 날 뻔했어요.

A serious accident nearly happened.

늦잠을 자서 회의에 늦을 뻔했어요.

I overslept and almost was late for the meeting. (늦 ends in ㅈ → 을)

앞차가 갑자기 멈춰서 하마터면 부딪칠 뻔했어요.

The car in front stopped suddenly, and I nearly crashed into it.

폭우로 인해 도로가 통제되었어요.

They suffered great damage because of the fire.

I tried to hold it in, but in the end I cried.

The result in the main clause holds true only while/if the condition in the first clause is met — "as long as X (is the case)". Attach 는 한 to the verb stem.

일회용품을 계속 쓰는 한 환경 문제는 해결되지 않아요.

As long as we keep using disposables, the environmental problem won't be solved.

건강이 허락하는 한 계속 일하고 싶어요.

As long as my health allows, I want to keep working.

규칙을 지키는 한 문제가 없을 거예요.

As long as you follow the rules, there will be no problem.

화석 연료에 의존하는 한 대기 오염을 완전히 막기는 어려워요.

As long as we depend on fossil fuels, it is hard to fully prevent air pollution.

-도록 — "so that / in order that / until"

Form Verb stem + 도록 (purpose, result, or extent)

Expresses a goal or result the action aims at ("so that…"), an instruction, or an extent ("to the point that…"). Attach 도록 to the verb stem.

환경이 오염되지 않도록 노력해야 해요.

We must make an effort so the environment isn't polluted. (purpose)

목이 아프도록 응원했어요.

I cheered until my throat was sore. (extent — "to the point that")

다른 사람이 알 수 있도록 안내문을 붙였어요.

I posted a notice so that others would know.

자원이 낭비되지 않도록 분리수거를 철저히 합시다.

Let's sort our recycling thoroughly so that resources aren't wasted.

-(으)므로 — "because / since (formal written)"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)므로 · N + (이)므로

A formal, written reason connector (reports, notices, essays). Attach -(으)므로 to the stem. Cannot take a command/suggestion in the second clause.

물가가 올랐으므로 가계 부담이 커졌어요.

Because prices rose, the household burden grew. (past 올랐 → 으므로)

예산이 부족하므로 지출을 줄여야 합니다.

Since the budget is insufficient, spending must be cut. (vowel stem 부족하 → 므로)

성실히 일했으므로 좋은 결과를 얻었어요.

Because they worked diligently, they got good results.

수요가 공급보다 많으므로 가격이 오를 것으로 보입니다.

Since demand exceeds supply, prices are expected to rise. (많 ends in ㄴ → 으므로)

Tip -(으)므로 is markedly formal and written — use 아서/어서 or 으니까 in everyday speech. Unlike 음으로(써) ("by means of"), 으므로 always states a reason.

은/는커녕 — "far from / let alone"

Form Noun + 은/는커녕 · also V/A + -기는커녕 (denies even the lesser case)

Not only is the bigger thing impossible — even a smaller, easier one is too. Attach 은/는커녕 to a noun (or -기는커녕 to a verb/adjective). Strongly negative, often with 도 in the second clause.

저축은커녕 생활비도 부족해요.

Far from saving, I don't even have enough for living costs. (저축 ends in ㄱ → 은커녕)

칭찬은커녕 혼만 났어요.

Far from praise, I only got scolded.

쉬기는커녕 밥 먹을 시간도 없었어요.

Let alone resting, I didn't even have time to eat. (verb 쉬다 → -기는커녕)

월급이 오르기는커녕 오히려 물가만 더 올랐어요.

Far from my salary going up, only prices rose even more.

LESSON 15법과 질서Law & Order

-다시피 — "as (you know / see)"

Form Verb stem + 다시피 (esp. 알다, 보다, 듣다: 알다시피, 보다시피)

Refers to what the listener already knows or can see — "as you know, as you can see". Attach 다시피 to the verb stem, mostly with perception/cognition verbs.

여러분도 알다시피 법은 모두가 지켜야 해요.

As you all know, the law must be obeyed by everyone.

보다시피 여기는 주차 금지 구역이에요.

As you can see, this is a no-parking zone.

앞에서 말했다시피 규칙이 바뀌었어요.

As I said earlier, the rules have changed.

뉴스에서 보셨다시피 음주 운전 처벌이 더 엄격해졌어요.

As you saw in the news, the punishment for drunk driving has become stricter.

Tip 다시피 pairs naturally with 알다, 보다, 듣다, 말하다 — verbs of knowing or perceiving. Don't confuse it with the look-alike 다시피 하다 ("practically do"), as in 굶다시피 하다 ("to all but starve").

-는 법이다 — "it is the way / it is natural that"

Form Verb stem + 는 법이다 · Adjective + -(으)ㄴ 법이다

States a general truth or principle — "that's how it works; it's only natural". Attach 는 법이다 to a verb stem (adjectives take -(으)ㄴ 법이다). Similar to 기 마련이다 but stresses a rule/principle.

죄를 지으면 벌을 받는 법이에요.

If you commit a crime, you get punished — that's the way it is.

노력한 만큼 얻는 법이에요.

You gain in proportion to your effort — that's how it works.

급하게 서두르면 실수하는 법이에요.

When you rush, you naturally make mistakes.

잘못을 솔직하게 인정하면 결국 신뢰를 얻는 법이에요.

If you honestly admit your mistakes, you end up earning trust — that's how it works.

Compare 는 법이다 and 기 마련이다 both state "it's only natural," but 는 법이다 frames it as a governing principle or rule, while 기 마련이다 simply notes an unavoidable tendency.

-(으)ㄹ지도 모르다 — "might / may possibly"

Form V/A + -(으)ㄹ지도 모르다 (past: -았/었을지도 모르다)

Expresses a possibility the speaker is uncertain about — "it might be that…, perhaps". Attach -(으)ㄹ지도 모르다 to the stem (past: -았/었을지도 모르다).

서류가 더 필요할지도 몰라요.

You might need more documents.

그분은 벌써 귀화했을지도 몰라요.

He may have already been naturalized. (past 귀화했 → -었을지도)

내일은 날씨가 추울지도 모르니까 옷을 챙기세요.

It might be cold tomorrow, so pack warm clothes.

처음에는 낯설어도 살다 보면 이곳이 제2의 고향이 될지도 몰라요.

It may feel unfamiliar at first, but as you live here, this place might become your second home.

N치고 — "for a…/considering it's a… (every / exception)"

Form Noun + 치고 (two senses: "every N without exception" or "(unusual) for an N")

Attach 치고 to a noun. Two senses: "every N is…" (치고 + negative = no exception), or "considering it's an N, (unexpectedly)…" with 치고는.

외국인치고 한국어를 정말 잘하시네요.

For a foreigner, you speak Korean really well.

초보치고는 운전을 잘하는 편이에요.

For a beginner, he drives quite well. ("unexpectedly good" sense → 치고는)

부모치고 자식 걱정 안 하는 사람 없어요.

There's no parent who doesn't worry about their child. ("every N" sense + negative)

이민 온 지 얼마 안 된 사람치고는 한국 생활에 정말 잘 적응했어요.

For someone who immigrated not long ago, she has adapted to life in Korea remarkably well.

Tip The two senses split by what follows: 치고 + a negative = "every N without exception"; 치고는 + a positive = "surprisingly good/different for an N."
