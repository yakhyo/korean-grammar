KIIP Level 4 — Grammar & Vocabulary

A self-study reference for 한국어와 한국문화 중급 2 (16-lesson edition) · Korea Immigration & Integration Program

This reference follows the KIIP Level 4 textbook (한국어와 한국문화 중급 2) — the upper-intermediate stage of the Social Integration Program. It is organized into the book's 16 lessons. At this level the topics shift toward society — science, heritage, globalization, the information age, elections, the economy, law, and immigrant life — and the grammar becomes more abstract: degree and emphasis, reported and quoted speech, supposition, concession, and formal written connectors. Each lesson presents its two grammar points (form, meaning, usage notes, examples) and matching vocabulary. A grammar index closes the book.

How to read each entry

V
Verb (동사) — e.g. 가다, 먹다
A
Adjective / descriptive verb (형용사) — e.g. 크다, 예쁘다
N
Noun (명사) — e.g. 사람, 학교
-(으)
Use -으 after a consonant-final stem; drop it after a vowel-final stem
아 / 어
Choose 아 after ㅏ/ㅗ stem vowels, otherwise 어 (하다 → 해)

LESSON 1한국 생활 적응Adapting to Life in Korea

-느라고 — "because of (doing), occupied with"

Form Verb stem + 느라고  ·  Rule same subject; second clause is usually a negative result

The effort or time spent on the first action causes a (usually unfavorable) result in the second — being unable to do something, being tired, missing out. Both clauses share one subject, and the second clause cannot be a command or suggestion.

한국 생활에 적응하느라고 정신이 없었어요.

I was so caught up adjusting to life in Korea that I was overwhelmed.

일하느라고 가족과 보낼 시간이 부족해요.

Being busy with work, I don't have enough time to spend with my family.

Note Compare 아서/어서: 느라고 stresses that time/effort spent on one action caused the negative outcome, and requires the same subject in both clauses.

-(으)ㄹ수록 — "the more… the more…"

Form consonant stem + 을수록 · vowel/ㄹ stem + ㄹ수록  ·  often 으면 + -(으)ㄹ수록

As the degree of the first situation increases, the second changes accordingly. Frequently reinforced by a leading 으면: 살면 살수록 ("the longer one lives…").

한국어는 공부할수록 재미있어요.

The more I study Korean, the more fun it gets.

한국에 살면 살수록 정이 들어요.

The longer I live in Korea, the more attached I become.

값이 쌀수록 품질을 잘 살펴봐야 해요.

The cheaper it is, the more carefully you should check the quality.

LESSON 2가족의 변화Changes in the Family

-(으)ㄹ 뿐만 아니라 — "not only… but also"

Form V/A + -(으)ㄹ 뿐만 아니라 · N + 뿐만 아니라 (past: -았/었을 뿐만 아니라)

Adds a second, often stronger point to the first. "Not only X, but also Y." Works with nouns directly (N뿐만 아니라).

요즘은 가족 형태가 다양할 뿐만 아니라 계속 변하고 있어요.

These days family forms are not only diverse but also constantly changing.

그분은 한국어뿐만 아니라 영어도 잘해요.

He is good not only at Korean but also at English.

이 일은 시간이 많이 걸릴 뿐만 아니라 비용도 많이 들어요.

This task not only takes a lot of time but also costs a lot.

-(으)ㄹ 수밖에 없다 — "have no choice but to / be bound to"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)ㄹ 수밖에 없다

There is no other option (with verbs), or an outcome is only natural and unavoidable (with adjectives).

맞벌이 가정이 늘면서 보육 시설이 늘어날 수밖에 없어요.

As dual-income households increase, childcare facilities are bound to grow.

버스가 끊겨서 택시를 탈 수밖에 없었어요.

The buses had stopped, so I had no choice but to take a taxi.

물가가 오르니 생활이 힘들 수밖에 없지요.

With prices rising, life is inevitably hard.

LESSON 3생활 속의 과학Science in Everyday Life

-(으)ㄴ/는 줄 알다 / 모르다 — "to (mistakenly) think / not know that"

Form V/A modifier + 줄 알다(모르다): 는 줄 (V present) · -(으)ㄴ 줄 (A / V past) · -(으)ㄹ 줄 (future)

Expresses what one assumed, knew, or failed to realize about a fact — often when the assumption turns out wrong. (Distinct from -(으)ㄹ 줄 알다 = "to know how to do".)

저는 그 기계가 고장 난 줄 몰랐어요.

I didn't know that machine was broken.

오늘 회의가 있는 줄 알았는데 아니었어요.

I thought there was a meeting today, but there wasn't.

이 약이 그렇게 효과가 좋은 줄 몰랐어요.

I had no idea this medicine was so effective.

-곤 하다 — "used to / tend to (repeatedly) do"

Form Verb stem + 곤 하다 (past habit: 곤 했다)

Describes a recurring or habitual action. In the past tense it means "used to (do) from time to time".

스마트폰이 없던 시절에는 편지를 쓰곤 했어요.

Back when there were no smartphones, I used to write letters.

스트레스를 받으면 단 음식을 찾곤 해요.

When I'm stressed, I tend to reach for sweet food.

어렸을 때 할머니 댁에 자주 가곤 했어요.

When I was young, I would often go to my grandmother's house.

LESSON 4한국의 의례Korean Ceremonies & Rites

-더니 — "(I observed)… and then / so now"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 더니 (recalls something witnessed; 2nd/3rd person)

Recalls something the speaker personally observed in the past, then states a related result or contrasting change. The subject is usually someone other than the speaker.

친구가 열심히 준비하더니 결국 시험에 합격했어요.

My friend prepared hard, and (sure enough) passed the exam.

아침에는 춥더니 지금은 따뜻해졌어요.

It was cold this morning, but now it's gotten warm.

동생이 한국 드라마를 보더니 한국어에 관심이 생겼어요.

My younger sibling watched Korean dramas and then took an interest in Korean.

-(으)ㄴ 나머지 — "as a result of (extreme)…"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)ㄴ 나머지 (the first state is intense/excessive)

Because of an extreme degree of emotion or state in the first clause, a (usually negative or unexpected) result follows.

너무 긴장한 나머지 인사말을 잊어버렸어요.

I was so nervous that I forgot my greeting.

기쁜 나머지 눈물이 났어요.

I was so happy that I cried.

서두른 나머지 중요한 서류를 두고 왔어요.

In my haste, I left an important document behind.

LESSON 5문화유산Cultural Heritage

얼마나 -(으)ㄴ/는지 모르다 — "you can't imagine how…"

Form 얼마나 + V/A + -(으)ㄴ지/는지 모르다 (A: -(으)ㄴ지 · V: -는지)

An emphatic expression stressing a high degree — "(it's) incredibly / you wouldn't believe how (much)…". The 모르다 keeps its literal "don't know" only loosely; the meaning is strong emphasis.

경복궁이 얼마나 아름다운지 몰라요.

You can't imagine how beautiful Gyeongbokgung is.

그 공연을 보고 얼마나 감동했는지 몰라요.

I was moved by that performance more than words can say.

한글이 얼마나 과학적인지 몰라요.

You'd be amazed how scientific Hangul is.

-든지 — "whether… or / no matter (which)"

Form V/A + 든지 (… 든지) · often with question words 누구, 무엇, 어디든지

Indicates a free choice or that the result is the same regardless of which option. Often paired (X든지 Y든지) or used with question words for "any-".

박물관에 가든지 고궁에 가든지 한 곳을 정합시다.

Whether we go to a museum or a palace, let's pick one place.

무엇을 하든지 최선을 다하세요.

Whatever you do, do your best.

언제든지 편하게 연락하세요.

Feel free to contact me anytime.

LESSON 6국제화 시대The Age of Globalization

-던 — "(the one) that used to / was being…"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + 던 + N (recalled, ongoing or incomplete past)

A past modifier recalling something habitual or unfinished in the past. Contrast with -았/었던 (a completed, more distant past state).

어렸을 때 자주 부르던 노래가 생각나요.

I remember a song I used to sing often as a child.

제가 마시던 커피가 어디 갔지요?

Where did the coffee I was drinking go?

예전에 조용하던 동네가 많이 변했어요.

The neighborhood that used to be quiet has changed a lot.

-(으)ㄹ 정도로 — "to the point/extent that"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)ㄹ 정도로 (also N + 정도로)

Describes the degree of something by giving a comparable extent — "so much that…, to the point of…".

눈물이 날 정도로 고향이 그리웠어요.

I missed my hometown to the point of tears.

발음이 한국 사람이라고 해도 될 정도로 좋아요.

Your pronunciation is good enough to pass for a Korean's.

셀 수 없을 정도로 많은 외국인이 한국에 살아요.

More foreigners than can be counted live in Korea.

LESSON 7현대인의 질병Illnesses of Modern Life

-되 — "do… but / provided that (formal)"

Form Verb stem + 되 (literary/formal; gives permission with a condition)

Allows or accepts the first action while attaching a condition or qualification in the second clause. Common in written or formal speech: "you may do X, but/provided Y."

운동을 하되 무리하지 마세요.

Do exercise, but don't overdo it.

약을 먹되 정해진 양을 지켜야 합니다.

You may take the medicine, but you must keep to the prescribed dose.

의견을 말하되 근거를 함께 제시하세요.

State your opinion, but present your reasons along with it.

-았/었더니 — "(I did)… and then (found/result)"

Form Verb stem + 았더니 / 었더니 (1st-person past action → discovery or result)

The speaker did something, and as a result discovered something or a consequence followed. The first clause is the speaker's own past action.

며칠 무리했더니 몸살이 났어요.

I overexerted myself for a few days, and then came down with body aches.

아침을 거르고 일했더니 어지러웠어요.

I worked through breakfast and ended up feeling dizzy.

꾸준히 운동했더니 건강이 좋아졌어요.

I exercised steadily, and my health improved.

LESSON 8정보화 사회The Information Society

-는다면서요? / -(으)ㄴ다면서요? — "I hear (that)…, right?"

Form V + -ㄴ/는다면서요? · A + 다면서요? · N + (이)라면서요? (confirming hearsay)

Confirms something the speaker has heard from someone else, asking the listener to verify. "I heard that…, is that true?"

요즘은 은행에 안 가고 휴대폰으로 송금한다면서요?

I hear that these days you transfer money by phone instead of going to the bank, right?

그 앱이 정말 편리하다면서요?

I heard that app is really convenient — is it?

다음 주부터 휴가라면서요?

I hear you're on vacation from next week, right?

-(으)ㄹ 겸 (-(으)ㄹ 겸) — "in order to… and also (dual purpose)"

Form Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 겸 (often doubled: A-(으)ㄹ 겸 B-(으)ㄹ 겸)

States that an action serves two purposes at once. Often used in pairs to list both aims.

정보도 찾을 겸 친구도 만날 겸 카페에 갔어요.

I went to the café both to look up information and to meet a friend.

바람도 쐴 겸 산책을 나갔어요.

I went out for a walk, partly to get some fresh air.

운동도 할 겸 지하철역까지 걸어가요.

I walk to the subway station, partly as exercise.

-(으)ㄹ 뻔하다 — "almost / nearly (happened)"

Form Verb stem + -(으)ㄹ 뻔하다 (always past: 뻔했다)

빙판길에서 넘어질 뻔했어요.

I almost slipped on the icy road.

A serious accident nearly happened.

폭우로 인해 도로가 통제되었어요.

Due to heavy rain, the road was closed.

부주의로 인한 사고가 많이 발생해요.

Many accidents occur because of carelessness.

화재로 인해 큰 피해를 입었어요.

They suffered great damage because of the fire.

LESSON 11교육 제도The Education System

Form Noun + 조차 (adds an extreme, often unexpected case; usually negative)

If you make an effort, your skills are bound to improve.

LESSON 12선거와 투표Elections & Voting

-나 보다 / -(으)ㄴ가 보다 — "(I guess / it seems) that…"

LESSON 13환경 보호Environmental Protection

-는 한 — "as long as / so long as"

Form Verb stem + 는 한 (states a condition that must hold)

The result in the main clause holds true only while/if the condition in the first clause is met — "as long as X (is the case)".

일회용품을 계속 쓰는 한 환경 문제는 해결되지 않아요.

As long as we keep using disposables, the environmental problem won't be solved.

건강이 허락하는 한 계속 일하고 싶어요.

As long as my health allows, I want to keep working.

규칙을 지키는 한 문제가 없을 거예요.

As long as you follow the rules, there will be no problem.

-도록 — "so that / in order that / until"

Form Verb stem + 도록 (purpose, result, or extent)

Expresses a goal or result the action aims at ("so that…"), an instruction, or an extent ("to the point that…").

환경이 오염되지 않도록 노력해야 해요.

We must make an effort so the environment isn't polluted.

목이 아프도록 응원했어요.

I cheered until my throat was sore.

다른 사람이 알 수 있도록 안내문을 붙였어요.

I posted a notice so that others would know.

LESSON 14생활과 경제Living & the Economy

-(으)므로 — "because / since (formal written)"

Form Verb / Adjective stem + -(으)므로 · N + (이)므로

A formal, written reason connector (reports, notices, essays). Cannot take a command/suggestion in the second clause.

물가가 올랐으므로 가계 부담이 커졌어요.

Because prices rose, the household burden grew.

예산이 부족하므로 지출을 줄여야 합니다.

Since the budget is insufficient, spending must be cut.

성실히 일했으므로 좋은 결과를 얻었어요.

Because they worked diligently, they got good results.

은/는커녕 — "far from / let alone"

Form Noun + 은/는커녕 · also V/A + -기는커녕 (denies even the lesser case)

Not only is the bigger thing impossible — even a smaller, easier one is too. Strongly negative, often with 도 in the second clause.

저축은커녕 생활비도 부족해요.

Far from saving, I don't even have enough for living costs.

칭찬은커녕 혼만 났어요.

Far from praise, I only got scolded.

쉬기는커녕 밥 먹을 시간도 없었어요.

Let alone resting, I didn't even have time to eat.

LESSON 15법과 질서Law & Order

-다시피 — "as (you know / see)"

Refers to what the listener already knows or can see — "as you know, as you can see". Mostly with perception/cognition verbs.

As you all know, the law must be obeyed by everyone.

For a beginner, he drives quite well.
